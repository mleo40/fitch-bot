The Lord of the Rings was published in three volumes between 1954 and 1955 by J.R.R. Tolkien.
Tolkien spent over 12 years writing The Lord of the Rings, starting in 1937.
The One Ring inscription reads: One Ring to rule them all, One Ring to find them, One Ring to bring them all and in the darkness bind them.
Tolkien invented the Elvish languages Quenya and Sindarin, giving them full grammar and vocabulary.
The Shire is modeled after the English countryside of Tolkien's childhood in Worcestershire.
Aragorn is 87 years old at the time of the War of the Ring - Dunedain age much slower than normal men.
Gandalf's full name in Valinor is Olorin. He was sent to Middle-earth as one of five Maiar wizards.
Sauron served under the dark lord Morgoth before becoming the primary villain of the Second and Third Ages.
The Eagles not flying the fellowship to Mordor is a common fan complaint - Tolkien addressed it: Sauron would have detected them.
Gollum's original name was Smeagol. He was a Stoor hobbit who lived over 500 years due to the Ring.
The Dead Marshes were inspired by Tolkien's experience in the Battle of the Somme during World War I.
Treebeard the Ent speaks the slowest language in Middle-earth - Entish takes a very long time to say anything.
Legolas and Gimli's friendship breaks a centuries-old enmity between Elves and Dwarves.
Minas Tirith was originally named Minas Anor - Tower of the Sun. Its sister city Minas Ithil became Minas Morgul.
The Balrog Durin's Bane lived in Moria for over 500 years before the dwarves awakened it in their mining.
Sam Gamgee is widely considered the true hero of The Lord of the Rings by Tolkien himself.
The Nazgul were once nine great kings of Men who accepted rings of power and were enslaved by Sauron.
Rivendell, home of Elrond, is called Imladris in Sindarin and has existed since the Second Age.
The Silmarillion covers the creation of Middle-earth and the First Age, including the origins of all evil.
Peter Jackson's film trilogy won 17 Academy Awards, including Best Picture for Return of the King.
The Return of the King film won all 11 of the Oscars it was nominated for - a record tied only by Ben-Hur and Titanic.
Tolkien was a professor of Anglo-Saxon at Oxford and drew heavily on Norse and Finnish mythology.
The Kalevala, Finnish national epic, was the primary inspiration for Tolkien's creation of Quenya Elvish.
Mount Doom is called Orodruin in Sindarin and Amon Amarth in Quenya, meaning Mountain of Fate.
Shelob is a descendant of Ungoliant, the primordial spider who helped Morgoth destroy the Two Trees of Valinor.
The Prancing Pony inn in Bree is where Frodo first meets Strider, later revealed as Aragorn.
Hobbits were invented by Tolkien for The Hobbit in 1937. The word came to him while grading student papers.
The Fellowship breaks at Amon Hen after Boromir tries to take the Ring from Frodo.
Tolkien wrote The Lord of the Rings as letters to his son Christopher serving in World War II.
The Elvish word for friend is mellon - the password to the Gates of Moria.
Saruman's real name is Curunir. He was the head of the Istari order before his corruption by Sauron.
The Rohirrim are inspired by the Anglo-Saxons. Their language, Rohirric, is actually Old English in the books.
Fangorn Forest is one of the oldest forests in Middle-earth, home to the Ents and Huorns.
The Grey Havens is the port from which Frodo, Gandalf, Bilbo, and the Elves sail to the Undying Lands.
Tom Bombadil appears in the books but not the films - Tolkien described him as the spirit of the land itself.
The Mirror of Galadriel shows possible futures, not certain ones - a distinction Galadriel emphasizes to Frodo.
Mordor means Black Land in Sindarin. It is surrounded by the Ash Mountains to the north and west.
The Shire calendar has 12 months and 365 days, nearly identical to the modern calendar - a detail Tolkien worked out in full.
Boromir and Faramir are sons of Denethor II, the Steward of Gondor, who went mad under Sauron's influence.
The Lord of the Rings has been translated into over 60 languages and sold over 150 million copies.
Tolkien considered the destruction of the Shire, not the Ring, to be the emotional climax of the story.
Arwen gives up her immortality to be with Aragorn, a sacrifice that mirrors the tale of Luthien and Beren.
The Two Towers refers to both Orthanc (Saruman's tower) and Barad-dur (Sauron's fortress).
Merry and Pippin grow taller than normal hobbits after drinking Ent-draughts in Fangorn Forest.
The Battle of Pelennor Fields is the largest battle in Middle-earth history, involving tens of thousands of combatants.
Eowyn slays the Witch-king because she is no man - fulfilling the prophecy that no man could kill him.
The Dwarves call their homeland Khazad-dum. After its fall it became known as Moria, meaning Black Pit.
Tolkien's fictional world has its own complete chronology spanning thousands of years of history.
The word hobbit has no clear etymology - Tolkien claimed it came to him fully formed.
Andy Serkis's performance capture as Gollum is considered a landmark in digital filmmaking history.
