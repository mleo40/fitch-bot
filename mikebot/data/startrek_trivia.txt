Star Trek first aired on NBC on September 8, 1966, created by Gene Roddenberry.
The original series was nearly cancelled after season 2; a fan letter campaign saved it.
Leonard Nimoy initially resisted the Vulcan salute  -  he invented it himself on set.
The communicator flip phone inspired the design of Motorola's StarTAC mobile phone.
Nichelle Nichols almost quit after season 1; Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. convinced her to stay.
The word 'Klingon' was first spoken in the original series episode 'Errand of Mercy' (1967).
DeForest Kelley's catchphrase 'He's dead, Jim' was said over 30 times across the series.
The Vulcan salute is based on a Jewish priestly blessing gesture Nimoy remembered as a child.
Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan is widely considered the best film in the franchise.
The holodeck first appeared in the animated series before being introduced in TNG.
Patrick Stewart was cast as Picard despite Roddenberry's initial resistance to a bald captain.
Worf is the longest-running Star Trek character, appearing across TNG, DS9, and Picard.
The Borg were originally conceived as insectoid creatures before being redesigned as cyborgs.
DS9 was the first Trek series not overseen directly by Gene Roddenberry.
Voyager's Doctor (EMH) was originally planned as a minor recurring character.
The Enterprise-D from TNG was destroyed in Star Trek: Generations (1994).
Quark's bar on DS9 was based on the cantina scene in Star Wars.
Majel Barrett played three different roles across Star Trek: Nurse Chapel, Lwaxana Troi, and the computer voice.
The Vulcan nerve pinch was invented by Leonard Nimoy who felt Spock wouldn't throw punches.
Star Trek coined the term 'Trekkies'  -  though many fans prefer 'Trekkers.'
George Takei came out publicly as gay in 2005, decades after playing Sulu.
Scotty's catchphrase 'Beam me up, Scotty' was never said exactly that way in the original series.
The Federation's Prime Directive prohibits interference with pre-warp civilizations.
Klingon is a fully developed constructed language with its own dictionary and grammar.
J.J. Abrams' 2009 Star Trek reboot created an alternate 'Kelvin' timeline.
Warp 10 in Voyager is defined as infinite velocity  -  achieving it causes de-evolution.
The USS Enterprise NCC-1701 was named after the NASA test shuttle Enterprise.
Nichelle Nichols later worked with NASA to recruit minority and female astronauts.
Data's cat on TNG was named Spot  -  originally male, later female, continuity be damned.
Star Trek: The Next Generation averaged 20 million viewers per episode at its peak.
The Tribbles episode ('The Trouble with Tribbles') is one of the most beloved in Trek history.
Michael Dorn (Worf) has more Star Trek appearances than any other actor in the franchise.
Spock's home planet Vulcan is said to orbit 40 Eridani A, a real star 16 light-years away.
The original series budget forced reuse of sets and props across many episodes.
Captain Janeway of Voyager was the first female lead captain in a main Trek series.
Deep Space Nine introduced serialized storytelling to Trek  -  unusual for its era.
The Cardassians were created for DS9 but first appeared in TNG's 'The Wounded.'
Seven of Nine's catsuit was so tight, actress Jeri Ryan required help to sit down.
Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country is an allegory for the fall of the Soviet Union.
The red shirt trope: 73% of crew deaths in TOS were red-shirted security officers.
Tasha Yar was killed off in TNG season 1 at actress Denise Crosby's own request.
The Mirror Universe was introduced in TOS episode 'Mirror, Mirror' and revisited in DS9.
Section 31, a rogue intelligence agency, first appeared in DS9 and recurred in Discovery.
Star Trek: Enterprise was set as a prequel, 100 years before the original series.
The Romulans and Vulcans share a common ancestry, revealed in TOS.
Sisko of DS9 is the only captain to be confirmed as a godlike Bajoran Prophet at series end.
LeVar Burton hosted Reading Rainbow for 26 years  -  and also played Geordi La Forge on TNG.
The Q Continuum in TNG was inspired partly by the Olympian gods of Greek mythology.
Captain Pike preceded Kirk as captain of the Enterprise in the unaired TOS pilot.
Star Trek has inspired real scientists  -  the name 'exoplanet' was partly popularized through Trek.
